Foil Racing League Launches as First US-Focused National Racing Circuit for Pump Foil and eFoil

A flatwater competitive league built to grow the sport — open to every foil, every brand, every rider.

MIAMI, FL — May 25, 2026 — The Foil Racing League (FRL) today announced its launch as the first US-focused competitive league dedicated to foil racing, spanning both pump foil (non-powered) and eFoil (powered) categories across a national circuit of American lakes.

FRL is built on three principles: US-focused, flatwater, inclusive by design. The league's mission is to give the rapidly growing American foiling community what it has never had — a professional national racing circuit that any rider can climb, regardless of which brand they ride or how they came to the sport.

"Foiling is one of the fastest-growing water sports in the country, but it has never had a national league of its own," said Jamie Finn, Co-Founder and CEO of the Foil Racing League. "We're building the league the sport deserves — open to every rider, every brand, every level. The goal is to grow this thing together. Inclusive at the grassroots, professional at the top."

Why flatwater, why US-focused

Flatwater offers predictable conditions, family-friendly access, and venues that work for spectators on the shore and racers on the course. The US has a deep network of foilable lakes across every region of the country, and an American foiling community that has reached the critical mass needed to support a national circuit.

FRL will run two distinct competitive categories — pump foil and eFoil — each with its own divisions across Pro, Open Amateur, Masters, Junior, and Women's classes. The league is race-only: GPS-verified time trials, head-to-head sprint brackets, and lap races on closed buoy courses. No freestyle, no judged formats. Every result is measured by the clock.

Brian Grubb joins as Co-Founder

Brian Grubb joins the Foil Racing League as a Co-Founder. Grubb has spent the last four years building and running the FSRL race series, developing race operations, athlete relations, and the format conventions that the broader foil racing world has come to share. He is one of the most experienced operators in competitive foiling, and brings that foundation to FRL as it scales the sport to a national stage.

"Brian's experience is invaluable to what we're building," said Finn. "Four years of running races, knowing the riders, refining the formats — you can't shortcut that. We're taking what works, learning from what doesn't, and building the next layer on top: a national circuit, professional operations, and a real pathway from a first race to the top of the sport."

Grubb's mandate at FRL covers tour operations, athlete development, and race format. Finn will lead the business side of the league — partnerships, sponsorships, technology, and growth.

Founding brand partners — and an open invitation

Lift Foils has signed on as the first founding brand partner of the Foil Racing League. The original eFoil brand brings deep product credibility across the category and a long-standing investment in the riders and communities that have built this sport.

FRL's partnership program is not closed and not exclusive. The league is actively inviting other foiling brands — boards, wings, propulsion, apparel, accessories — to join the founding partner group. The goal is to grow the sport collectively, not to pick winners.

"This league is bigger than any one brand, including ours," said Finn. "Lift stepped up first, and we're grateful. But the door is wide open. If you build foiling gear and you want to see this sport grow in the US, we want you in the room."

About the Foil Racing League

The Foil Racing League (FRL) is a US-focused competitive foiling league spanning the pump foil and eFoil categories, organized around a national flatwater circuit. Race-only, family-friendly, and inclusive by design, FRL is building the competitive pathway American foiling has never had — from first race to national tour to a future world championship.

Headquartered in Miami, FL. Learn more at foilracingleague.com.
