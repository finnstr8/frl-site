THE FORMAT

Two categories,
four disciplines, one lake.

NON-POWERED
Pump foil
  • 1v1 Sprint Bracket — head-to-head, 200–400m closed course
  • Lap Race — multi-lap endurance on a buoy course
  • Freestyle / Best Trick — judged expression session
  • Foil Assist Division — battery-assisted pump for newer riders
POWERED
eFoil
  • 1v1 Sprint Bracket — short course, head-to-head finals
  • Time Trial — GPS-timed solo runs against the clock
  • Freestyle / Best Trick — judged expression session
  • Brand Cup Classes — Lift Cup, Flite Cup, optional
04
THE WEEKEND DESIGN

Everyone races. All weekend.

01

Pool play → brackets

Saturday: round-robin in pools of 4–6. Sunday: top finishers seed into main brackets, everyone else drops into a consolation bracket. Every entry guarantees water time both days.

02

Multi-discipline scoring

One entry covers all your division's events — sprint, lap race, freestyle. A bad sprint Saturday doesn't end your weekend; you can still chase points across other disciplines and the season.

03

Sunday team relay finale

Captains draft mixed rosters of pro, amateur, junior, and women's riders across categories. Pros want to win it. Amateurs want to be drafted. Spectators love it. Always run last.

THE BUILD PATH

Earn the right
to call it a tour.

YEAR 0

Pilot

One venue, one weekend. Two disciplines if needed. Prove the format. Capture media. Build sponsor case study.

YEAR 1

Anchor

Full four-discipline format at one venue. Lock title sponsor. Publish class rules and points formula.

YEAR 2

Mini series

Two to three stops. Points-based season title. Year-end championship at strongest venue.

YEAR 3+

Full tour

Eight stops. Class associations. Athlete rankings. Broadcast partner. Pursue NGB recognition.

Don't announce a league. Announce one event. Run it well. Earn the rest.
