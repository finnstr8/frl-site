04
THE FORMAT

Two categories. Pure racing.

NON-POWERED
Pump foil
  • 1v1 Sprint Bracket — head-to-head, 200–400m closed course
  • Lap Race — multi-lap endurance on a buoy course
  • Time Trial — GPS-timed solo runs against the clock
  • Foil Assist Division — battery-assisted pump for newer riders
POWERED
eFoil
  • 1v1 Sprint Bracket — short course, head-to-head finals
  • Time Trial — GPS-timed solo runs against the clock
  • Lap Race — multi-lap endurance on a buoy course
  • Brand Cup Classes — Lift Cup, Flite Cup, optional
05
THE WEEKEND DESIGN

Everyone races. All weekend.

01

Pool play → brackets

Saturday: round-robin sprint qualifiers in pools of 4–6. Sunday: top finishers seed into main brackets, everyone else drops into a consolation bracket. Every entry guarantees water time both days.

02

Multi-format scoring

One entry covers your division's full slate — sprint bracket, lap race, and time trial. A bad sprint Saturday doesn't end your weekend; you can still chase points in lap races and time trials, and across the season.

03

Sunday wraps by lunch

Finals run mid-morning, awards at 12:30, closing lunch by 1:00 PM. Riders fly home Sunday afternoon, spectators get a clean half-day, sponsors get a tight broadcast window. Respect everyone's time.

THE BUILD PATH

Four years
to a world championship.

YEAR 1

US launch

Eight US stops. Pump foil + eFoil. Lock founding members. Publish class rules. Launch FRL app MVP. Establish the season points format.

YEAR 2

US scale

Ten US stops. Full pro qualification system live via the app. Broadcast partner secured. Athlete rankings published nationally.

YEAR 3

International

Expand into Europe, Australia, and Asia-Pacific via partner-run regional series. Continental qualifiers feed into a global leaderboard.

YEAR 4

Worlds

Inaugural FRL World Championship at Lake Tahoe. Top qualifiers from every region. Rotates to a new global venue each year thereafter.
